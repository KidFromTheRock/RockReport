Welcome Letter

The Path Starts Here

Welcome to the first issue of The Rock Report, the weekly newsletter from Kid From The Rock.

I’m Keiki Misipeka, founder of Kid From The Rock and former Director of Player Personnel & NIL Engagement at the University of Miami. After years in college football, I launched this service to help student-athletes and their families better understand today’s recruiting landscape.

The transfer portal and NIL have changed the game. Families need trusted guidance, honest education and people who can help them ask the right questions before big decisions get made.

The Rundown
  • Get Seen: recruiting exposure, profiles, film and opportunities.
  • Build the Player: training, nutrition, eligibility and the college sports ecosystem.

  • From the Rock: profiles, rankings, area-code spotlights and pipeline stories.
What We’ll Cover
Get Seen

Recruiting Starts With Visibility

The short view:
Talent matters, but exposure matters too. We’ll help families understand how athletes can improve their recruiting profiles, build better film, find scholarship opportunities and get in front of the right people.

Why it matters: More clarity means fewer wasted camps, fewer bad assumptions and a better chance for the right coaches to find the right kids.

Build the Player

College-Ready Means More Than Game-Ready

The short view:
This section will cover development from every angle — training, nutrition, recovery, eligibility, NCAA rules, the recruiting calendar, the transfer portal and what families need to understand about the college sports ecosystem.

Why it matters: The new 5-for-5 model and a faster roster marketplace make it even more important for families to understand the timeline early.

Money Game

Before the First NIL Check, Have the First Family Conversation

NIL has created real opportunity, but it has also created real confusion. This section will focus on education: what NIL is, what it is not, what families should expect, and how athletes can think about money, brand value and long-term decisions the right way.

The short view:
Most athletes will not get big NIL money right away. But every family should understand the basics before money enters the conversation.

We’ll celebrate athletes from American Samoa, players of Polynesian descent across the mainland, former players, coaches, families and the people carrying the culture forward.

Before You Go
  • •For families: Start with the basics — current film, accurate measurables, academic info and a clear list of target schools.
  • •For athletes: Your recruiting profile is not a one-time upload. Treat it like a living resume.
