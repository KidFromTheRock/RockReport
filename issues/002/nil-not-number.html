Money Game

NIL Is More Than a Number

Money Game presented by The Seiler Group of Raymond James

When families hear “NIL,” it is easy to think first about dollar amounts. That is natural. But the smarter conversation starts before the number.

The current NIL landscape includes school-related compensation, collective or marketing-driven opportunities and corporate sponsor deals. Some opportunities may be reviewed for fair market value and compliance. Some may sound bigger than they are. Some may come with obligations that families do not fully understand at first glance.

That is why NIL education has to include more than “how much?”

Questions before the deal

  • What is the athlete being asked to do? Social posts, appearances, camps, autographs, content, endorsements and exclusivity terms all matter.
  • Who is paying? A school-related source, collective, local business and national brand can each come with different expectations.
  • What rights are being used? Name, image, likeness, logo usage, content rights and contract length should be clear.
  • What could affect eligibility? Compliance review matters. So does understanding what is allowed by the school, conference, NCAA rules and state law.
  • What happens after payment? Taxes, saving, family expectations and long-term planning should not be afterthoughts.

The short view: A good NIL conversation protects the athlete first. The number matters, but the terms matter more.

Families do not need to become experts overnight. They do need to slow down enough to ask better questions and bring in qualified help before signing anything they do not understand.
