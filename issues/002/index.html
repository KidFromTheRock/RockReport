This Week

The Calendar Is Real. The Path Is Bigger Than One School.

Recruiting has windows. Player development has layers. NIL has real responsibility. This week, we’re looking at what families should understand before the next opportunity shows up.

Issue 001 introduced the mission of Kid From The Rock. Issue 002 starts the weekly rhythm: translate the recruiting landscape, build smarter athletes and families, and spotlight the Polynesian pipeline from today’s players to the legends who opened doors.

The Rundown
  • Get Seen: why the NCAA recruiting calendar should shape your family plan.

  • Money Game: why NIL conversations should start before the number.
  • From the Rock: Talanoa Hufanga today, Troy Polamalu forever.
Get Seen

Recruiting Has a Calendar. Families Need to Know It.

The short view:
NCAA recruiting calendars help define when and how coaches may recruit prospective student-athletes. Those windows should shape when families update film, plan visits, choose camps and prepare for communication.

Why it matters: Waiting until a coach reaches out is not a plan. Families should know when the process can move before the moment arrives.

Build the Player

The Four Jobs Every College Football Player Has to Manage

The short view:
The college athlete’s life is built around academics, football, professional obligations and social life. The athletes who last are usually the ones who learn how to prioritize before the schedule gets heavy.

Why it matters: College-ready means more than game-ready. The routine is part of the jump.

Before You Go
  • •Check the calendar: Find the recruiting calendar for the athlete’s sport, class year and target division.
  • •Update the profile: Current film, accurate measurables, transcripts and contact information should be ready before windows open.
  • •Ask before signing: NIL and representation documents should be reviewed by qualified help before families commit.
  • •Culture note: Aiga, nu‘u, fa‘atura and tautua are not just words. They are the reason the path matters.
